PRURITUS ANI

Description: Pruritus ani is the medical term for persistent itching around the anus.

Common Causes:

  • Skin irritation from moisture or friction
  • Hemorrhoids or anal fissures
  • Bacterial or fungal infections
  • Certain foods (spicy, acidic, or caffeinated)

Treatment Options:

  • Improved hygiene practices
  • Topical medications (steroids or antifungals)
  • Dietary modifications
  • Treatment of underlying conditions

When to See a Surgeon:

If symptoms persist despite conservative treatment, or if you notice bleeding, lumps, or other concerning symptoms.

PERI-ANAL ABSCESS

Description: A painful collection of pus near the anus caused by infection.

Symptoms:

  • Painful swelling near the anus
  • Redness and warmth in the area
  • Fever in some cases
  • Pain with bowel movements

Treatment:

Most peri-anal abscesses require surgical drainage under local or general anesthesia.

Recovery:

Typically takes 2-4 weeks. May require wound packing and antibiotics.
